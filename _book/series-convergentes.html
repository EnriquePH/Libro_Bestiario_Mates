3.1 Funciones de parte entera

3.1.1 Función techo de \(n^2\) medios, A000982:

\[A000982(n) = \{0, 1, 2, 5, 8, 13, 18, 25, 32, 41, 50, 61, 72, 85, 98, 113, 128, 145, 162, 181, 200,...\}\] \[\begin{equation} A000982(n) = \left\lceil \frac{n^2}{2} \right\rceil \tag{3.1} \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} A001105(n) = 2n^2 \tag{3.2} \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} A001844(n) = 2n(n+1) + 1 \tag{3.3} \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{\left\lceil \frac{n^2}{2} \right\rceil}} = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{2m^2}} + \sum_{m=0}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{2m(m+1) + 1}} \tag{3.4} \end{equation}\]

Para sumar la serie infinita, separamos la secuencia en dos una con los términos pares y la otra con los impares.

\[\begin{equation} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{\left\lceil \frac{n^2}{2} \right\rceil}} = \frac{\zeta(2)}{2} + \frac{\pi}{2} \cdot \tanh(\frac{\pi}{2}) \tag{3.5} \end{equation}\]

3.1.2 Función suelo de \(n^2\) tercios, A000212:

\[A000212(n) = \{0, 0, 1, 3, 5, 8, 12, 16, 21, 27, 33, 40, 48, 56, 65, 75,...\}\] \[\begin{equation} A000212(n) = \left\lfloor \frac{n^2}{3} \right\rfloor \tag{3.6} \end{equation}\]

Separamos la secuencia problema, en tres secuencias, para sumarlas por separado

  • Números octagonales: A000567:

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} \left\lfloor \frac{(3m+2)^{2}}{3} \right\rfloor \\ & = A000567(m) \\ & = \{1, 8, 21, 40, 65, 96, 133, 176, 225, 280, 341, 408, 481, 560,...\} \\ & = m(3m-2) \end{split} \tag{3.7} \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \sum_{m=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{m(3m-2)}} = \frac{1}{12} (\sqrt{3}\pi + 9\log{3}) \tag{3.8} \end{equation}\]

  • Números rómbicos: A045944

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} \left\lfloor \frac{(3m+1)^{2}}{3} \right\rfloor \\ & = A045944(m) \\ & = \{5, 16, 33, 56, 85, 120, 161, 208, 261, 320, 385, 456, 533,...\} \\ & = m(3m+2) \end{split} \tag{3.9} \end{equation}\]

  • Secuencia \(3n^2\) A033428:

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} \left\lfloor \frac{(3m)^{2}}{3} \right\rfloor \\ & = A033428(m) \\ & = \{3, 12, 27, 48, 75, 108, 147, 192, 243, 300, 363, 432, 507,...\} \\ & = 3m^2 \end{split} \tag{3.10} \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \sum_{m=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{3m^2}} = \frac{\zeta(2)}{3} \tag{3.11} \end{equation}\]

3.1.3 Cuartos cuadrados,1 secuencia A002620

\[A002620(n) = \{0, 0, 1, 2, 4, 6, 9, 12, 16, 20, 25, 30, 36, 42, 49, 56,...\}\] Se puede observar como los términos pares de la secuencia se corresponden a los cuadrados perfectos (A000290), mientras que los términos impares son los números oblongos (A002378).

\[\begin{equation} A002620(n) = \left\lfloor \frac{n^2}{4} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{n^2}{2} \right\rfloor \cdot \left\lceil \frac{n^2}{2} \right\rceil \tag{3.12} \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} \sum_{n=2}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{A002620(n)}} & = \sum_{n=2}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{ \left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor}} \\ & = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{m^2}} + \sum_{m=1}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{m(m+1)}} \\ & = \zeta(2) + 1 \\ & = \frac{\pi^2}{6} + 1 \end{split} \tag{3.13} \end{equation}\]

3.1.4 Generalización

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} S(k) & = \sum_{n=\lceil \sqrt{k} \rceil}^{\infty}{\frac{1}{\left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{k}\right\rfloor}} \\ & = \sum_{s=0}^{k-1}{\sum_{m=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\left\lfloor\frac{{(k m + s)}^{2}}{k}\right\rfloor}} \\ & = \frac{\zeta(2)}{k} + \sum_{s=1}^{k-1}{\sum_{m=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\left\lfloor\frac{{(k m + s)}^{2}}{k}\right\rfloor}} \end{split} \tag{3.14} \end{equation}\]
